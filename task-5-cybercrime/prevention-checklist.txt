Stay Safe Online Checklist for college students in India

1. Set all your social media accounts to private mode and review privacy settings every month to control who can see your photos, location tags, and personal details shared with college friends.
 
2. Never accept friend requests or chat with strangers on Instagram, Facebook, or dating apps, and avoid sharing your college timetable, hostel room number, or travel plans online.

3. Create strong, unique passwords for every online account using a password manager and enable two-factor authentication to block unauthorized logins.

4. Do not click on unknown links or download files from emails, WhatsApp messages, or social media posts, even if they claim to be from your college or friends.

5. Keep your smartphone and laptop locked with biometric authentication or a strong PIN, install reliable antivirus software, and keep all apps and operating systems updated regularly.

6. Think twice before posting any content online and avoid sharing compromising photos or videos that could be edited or misused by others.

7. For UPI safety never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or banking details with anyone via call, message, or email, no matter who they claim to be such as bank officials or college mates.

8. For additional financial protection always verify the exact name and mobile number of the recipient before approving any UPI transaction and enable SMS or app notifications for all payments to detect fraud quickly.

9. Avoid using public Wi-Fi networks for banking, shopping, or accessing college email and use your mobile data or a trusted VPN when necessary instead.

10. If you encounter any cyberbullying, fraud, or suspicious activity report it immediately through the National Cyber Crime Portal at cybercrime.gov.in or by calling the helpline number 1930 for prompt assistance from authorities.
