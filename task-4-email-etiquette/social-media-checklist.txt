Responsible Social Media Use Checklist for College Students

Do’s  
1. Set your accounts to private and review privacy settings monthly so only trusted friends and classmates can view your posts, stories, and tagged photos.  
2. Pause and think before posting—ask yourself whether the content could affect your reputation, placements, or relationships with professors and peers.  
3. Share helpful academic resources, event updates, or study tips to build a positive online network within your college community.  
4. Verify facts before sharing any news, memes, or college-related information to stop the spread of misinformation.  
5. Schedule regular digital detox breaks to focus on studies, sleep, and face-to-face interactions with friends.  
6. Report bullying, harassment, or inappropriate content immediately using the platform’s tools and the National Cyber Crime Portal.

Don’ts  
1. Never post your daily college schedule, hostel room number, travel plans, or personal contact details that strangers could misuse.  
2. Do not engage in heated arguments or spread negativity in comments, especially with fellow students or faculty.  
3. Never share photos or videos of classmates, professors, or college events without their clear permission.  
4. Do not accept friend requests or follow unknown accounts on Instagram, Facebook, or LinkedIn.  
5. Avoid scrolling or posting during lectures, library hours, or exam preparation time to prevent academic distraction.  
6. Never use social media to mock, tag, or target anyone in a way that could be considered cyberbullying or harassment.
